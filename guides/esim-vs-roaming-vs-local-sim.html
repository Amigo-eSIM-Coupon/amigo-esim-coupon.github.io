eSIM vs Roaming vs Local SIM – Which Is Best?

A complete comparison of your mobile data options while traveling. We break down the cost, convenience, and coverage of each approach.

The Three Options Explained

When traveling internationally, you have three main ways to get mobile data: international roaming on your home plan, a travel eSIM, or a local physical SIM card. Each has its pros, cons, and ideal use cases.

Option 1: International Roaming

International roaming means using your home carrier's network while abroad. Your home carrier partners with local networks to give you access, but passes on the cost — often at a significant markup.

Pros

  • Zero setup — works automatically
  • Keep your home phone number
  • Receive calls/texts normally

Cons

  • Very expensive — often $10–$15/day
  • Data caps are very low per price
  • Bill shock is common
  • Speeds can be throttled

Best for: Very short trips (1–2 days) where convenience outweighs cost, or business travelers with company-covered roaming plans.

Option 2: Travel eSIM

A travel eSIM is a digital SIM card that you install on your compatible smartphone. You purchase a plan from a travel eSIM provider like Amigo eSIM and activate it via QR code — no physical card needed. It runs alongside your existing SIM so you keep your home number.

Pros

  • Instant digital delivery — no waiting at counters
  • Keep your home SIM active simultaneously
  • Works in 150+ countries
  • Competitive local data rates
  • No physical card to lose or swap
  • 12% off with coupon ESIMDUDE

Cons

  • Requires eSIM-compatible device
  • Usually data-only (no local phone number)
  • Slightly more expensive than local SIM

Best for: Most international travelers — especially multi-country trips, short-to-medium stays, and anyone who wants to keep their home number active.

Option 3: Local SIM Card

Buying a local physical SIM card at your destination gives you local rates and often the cheapest per-GB costs. However, it requires you to remove your home SIM, find a carrier store, and deal with language barriers.

Pros

  • Cheapest per-GB rates
  • Full local network access
  • Often includes voice/SMS

Cons

  • Must find a store on arrival
  • Language barriers in some countries
  • Lose your home number while using it
  • Physical card can be lost or damaged
  • Not practical for multi-country trips

Best for: Long-term stays (1+ month) in a single country where cost efficiency matters most.
