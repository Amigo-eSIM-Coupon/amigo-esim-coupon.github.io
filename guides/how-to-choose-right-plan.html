How to Choose the Right Amigo eSIM Plan

Not sure how much data you need? This guide walks you through choosing the perfect plan for any trip — with a data usage calculator and expert tips.

Updated April 2026  ·  6 min read  ·  Works for all destinations

Step 1 – Know Your Destination

Different regions have different plan pricing. Check Amigo eSIM's plan page for your destination country. Common regional categories are Europe, Asia, Americas, Middle East, and Global (multi-region).

Step 2 – Estimate Your Data Usage

Use the table below to estimate how much data common activities use per hour:

Activity Data per Hour Notes
Google Maps navigation ~15–30 MB Higher with satellite view
WhatsApp / Messaging ~5–10 MB Text + occasional images
Social media browsing ~100–300 MB Instagram/TikTok heavier
Video calls (HD) ~500–700 MB Zoom, FaceTime, etc.
Music streaming ~60–150 MB Spotify standard quality
Video streaming (SD) ~300–500 MB Netflix standard
Video streaming (HD) ~1–3 GB Netflix HD / YouTube HD
Email / light browsing ~5–20 MB Text-heavy sites

Step 3 – Match to a Plan Size

1 GB Plan

Perfect for a weekend trip (2–3 days) with basic navigation and messaging. Avoid video streaming.

Best for: Short city breaks

3 GB Plan

Good for a 5–7 day trip with regular Maps use, social media browsing, and occasional video calls.

Best for: Week-long holidays

5 GB Plan

Comfortable for a 7–14 day trip. Allows some streaming and regular video calls without worry.

Best for: Two-week trips

10 GB Plan

Ideal for longer trips or heavy users. Remote workers, frequent video callers, or streaming lovers.

Best for: Remote workers & long stays

Step 4 – Choose the Right Duration

Amigo eSIM plan validity typically ranges from 7 to 30 days from first activation. Choose a plan duration that covers your full trip. If your trip is 10 days, a 15-day plan gives you a buffer. You can always top up if needed — no need to buy a new plan.

💡 Pro Tip: It's better to buy slightly more data than you think you need. If you run out mid-trip, you'll spend more buying an emergency top-up than if you'd bought the next size up from the start.

Data-Saving Tips Abroad

  • Download offline maps before you leave (Google Maps, Maps.me)
  • Use Low Data Mode (iPhone) or Data Saver (Android)
  • Stream music and podcasts offline via Spotify or similar apps
  • Use WiFi at hotels, cafes, and airports for heavy tasks
  • Turn off automatic app updates and background refresh
  • Use WhatsApp or iMessage over data for free messaging (instead of SMS)
